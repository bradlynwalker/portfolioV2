Project:Magika

Making the ephemeral, memorable.

My Role:

I was the research lead for this project. I conducted the literature reviews and competitive analyses. I also led the usability testing, the subsequent data analysis, and led presentation of findings from our research.


Background

What defines a moment in your life? Is it who you spent that moment with? Or the context in which it took place? Our team's challenge was to find something in the space of creativity and apply user centered design principles. Through several sitdown sessions, we decided that music would be our problem space. Music is something that's deeply personal to many people and functions as both an outlet and impetus for creativity.

Initial Research and Ideation

The challenge that was imposed on our team was to make actionable research and design decisions right out of the gate. We studied psychology literature to better understand the role that music plays in people's lives. The more we poured over the literature, the more we gravitated towards the role of music in a person's life story. According to Belfi, Karlan, and Tranel (2015), music can evoke vivid autobiographical memories. It can be used to evoke "the sights, sounds, and feelings of a specific event". From a Psychological perspective, JÃ¤ncke (2008) notes that "listening to music (even when we listen passively) activates many psychological functions (emotion, memory, attention, imagery and so on)." This idea of an event, or "moment" inspired us. We can store different aspects of a moment right now such as photos on Facebook, or places we've checked into on Google, but a moment is greater than the sum of its parts. To us, a moment is comprised of a mood, a location, and how the music supports how you feel at that point in time, and the design we would come up with would have to facilitate all of those to the best our ability. This is where we started our ideation.

From our research and some guerrilla-style interviews, we drafted four archetypes:

Our ideation centered around mobility-based ways to experience moments. For our music component, we wanted to interface with Spotify to do much of the heavy lifting for the music services. According to Spotify, 60% of listening is done on mobile devices. Being able to record a moment was essential to the experience we wanted to deliver, but another part of this idea was the ability to discover the moments of others.

Idea 1

Here we explored the idea where users can drop songs at their current location during playback. This would be a fairly quick lightweight action, in hopes of encouraging people to drop as many songs as they want to.

Idea 2

And here we explored the idea where users happen across songs in the middle of doing something else, such as strolling through the park. They have the option to just collect the songs or to listen to them immediately.

The platform that Jordan and I wanted to create needed to service these two use cases with equivalent strength. As we found through the construction of our archetypes, there are different types of motivations under the labels of content creators and content consumers. Magika needed to support both instantaneous and retroactive moment drops as well as guided and unguided discovery of moments. This led us to five core functions that would be built into Magika:

  1. 1. Create a Moment on the spot from the song currently playing.
  2. 2. Create a 'Moment' from a song on a prior Journey.
  3. 3. Using a map to actively discover Moments.
  4. 4. Looking back at previously discovered and created Moments'
  5. 5. Rating a discovered Moment in terms of personal preference and others likelihood to enjoy the song.

Prototype

Navigation

 Utilizing Google Maps API, users can navigate to areas where they want to discover moments, then find specific moments within that area to discover. Alternatively, they can allow themselves to be surprised and discover moments by happenstance as they go about their activities. This is part of the joy factor of Magika.

Rating

 Users are informed when they discover a Moment with a push notification so it's easily dismissed if need be. If the users listens to the song, they can offer feedback on whether quality of the Moment as well as whether others might find the Moment effective.

Moment Creation

 Users can return to previous Journeys at any time to review the songs they listened to on Spotify. If there's a song that speaks to them from their Journey, they can easily tag and place the song and continue on their way.
