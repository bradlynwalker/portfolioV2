Project:Magika

Making the ephemeral, memorable.

My Role:

I was the research lead for this project. I conducted the literature reviews and competitive analyses. I also led the usability testing, the subsequent data analysis, and led presentation of findings from our research.

Background

What defines a moment in your life? Is it who you spent that moment with? Or the context in which it took place? Our team's challenge was to find something in the space of creativity and apply user centered design principles. Through several sitdown sessions sessions, we decided that music would be our problem space. Music is something that's deeply personal to many people and functions as both an outlet and impetus for creativity.

Initial Research and Ideation

The challenge that was imposed on our team was to make actionable research and design decisions right out of the gate. We studied psychology literature to better understand the role that music plays in people's lives. The more we poured over the literature, the more we gravitated towards the role of music in a person's life story. According to Belfi, Karlan, and Tranel (2015), music can evoke vivid autobiographical memories. It can be used to evoke "the sights, sounds, and feelings of a specific event". From a Psychological perspective, JÃ¤ncke (2008) notes that "listening to music (even when we listen passively) activates many psychological functions (emotion, memory, attention, imagery and so on)"." This idea of an event, or "moment" inspired us. We can store different aspects of a moment right now such as photos on Facebook, or places we've checked into on Google, but a moment is greater than the sum of its parts. To us, a moment is comprised of a mood, a location, and how the music supports how you feel at that point in time, and the design we would come up with would have to facilitate all of those to the best our ability. This is where we started our ideation.

From our research and some guerrila-style interviews, we drafted four archetypes:

Our ideation centered around mobility-based ways to experience moments. For our music component, we wanted to interface with Spotify to do much of the heavy lifting for the music services. According to Spotify, 60% of listening is done on mobile devices. Being able to record a moment was essential to the experience we wanted to deliver, but another part of this idea was the ability to discover the moments of others.

Goal: Gather the required amount of points to complete the game or survive for 5-10 minutes, whichever comes first.

In addition, we also asked questions about the heads-up display on the screen and whether it contained the necessary information the player needs to execute strategies during the game.

Findings

Problem Action Item
Players struggled to kill enemies in the game, meaning players scored few, if any points. Reduce difficulty of enemies, possibly increase damage output of player character.
Players often went negative in point count due to gameplay elements that took points away Reduce penalties for errors in gameplay, possibly increase point accumulation for correct gameplay.
Players were frustrated by the inability to attack while moving Implement the ability to attack enemies while moving in a given direction
Parts of the level were not obviously marked in the environment, participants did not explore or discover the other half of the level. Rework visual elements to make hallways and paths to other areas more visible, reduce the amount environmental objects obstruct screen space.

We compiled these findings and action items into a document which guided the direction of game development until completion. We ended up getting a 104/100 on this project! Not too bad!

About the game

Chromaga is a third person hack-and-slash game. You play as the white pixel; you're trying to escape the television that's held you captive your entire life. In your way stand your brothers and sisters who have been turned against you by the gatekeeper of the television. As you defeat enemies, you carry their essences with you until the gatekeeper himself decides to emerge and deal with you himself. Will you win you freedom?
